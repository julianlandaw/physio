where Vd, central is the volume of distribution of the central compartment, Vd, peripheral is the volume of distribution of the peripheral compartment, b is the amount bolused into the compartment (e.g. via an IV infusion), Cl is the clearance, and k is the distribution rate between the two compartments.

In a two compartment model, there are two sources of a drug escaping the central compartment - distribution (i.e. movement to the peripheral compartment) and elimination. The rates of these are governed by k and Cl. It can be shown that the half-lives of distribution and elimination are (k ln(2))/Vd, central and (Cl ln(2))/Vd, central, respectively, which are termed t1/2,a and t1/2,b.

Three Compartment Model
